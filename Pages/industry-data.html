IT Support Officer

An IT Support Officer is responsible for supporting all aspects of the IT systems and services. They do this by monitoring, maintaining, and providing technical assistance for an organisation's IT systems. It can also be done remotely, supporting users through remote desktop software and telephone and email conversations. Some tasks or duties may include technical user support, network administration, assessing issues, and hardware and software installation.

Top-general skills

Communication is important for this role as IT Supports Officers should have strong written and verbal communication and exhibit excellent listening skills. They must also display confidence when communicating with people such as staff and clients and demonstrate a high level of professionalism. They require the ability to identify and investigate issues and prioritise their requirements and the appropriate solutions; this means they must have strong problem-solving and time-management skills. IT Supports Officers should also have the skill of being flexible and the ability to adapt and adjust to changing processes. With this also comes the flexibility of working with different people, at different hours and within different time frames.

IT-specific skills

IT support officers should have include efficiently setting up hardware and software by setting up equipment, ensuring proper installation, and performing cables and operating systems or the appropriate software. They should also be able to maintain records of daily data communication with problems, remedial actions taken, and installation activities.



Cyber Security Analyst

Cyber security analysts are also called information security analysts. They protect organisations from cyber threats and develop protocols to provide solutions to respond to cyber-attacks and improve their ICT security. Their jobs are vital to protecting the infrastructure, such as hardware devices and computer networks, from hackers and cybercriminals seeking to cause damage or steal information. Generally, their duties are researching the latest security trends, analysing current software security systems' data, and developing strategies to improve data protection.

Top-general skills

Cyber Security Analysts should have strong critical thinking and problem-solving abilities, as most of their job includes troubleshooting and finding solutions to possible issues. To be a successful cyber security analyst, you must also be able to adapt quickly to new threats and different types of technologies; however, you must also be able to identify and manage risks. Good research skills and conceptual understanding of systems and networks will help a cyber security analyst stay up to date with current challenges and be able to respond promptly to threats or interruptions.

IT-specific skills

To become a successful cyber security analyst, it is important to have experience implementing and managing cloud computing systems and processes. It is to help protect the business's data storage. Cybersecurity analysts must also understand essential software and technology skills, such as firewall installation, network configuration, and programming.



Software Engineer

A Software engineer applies engineering principles and programming language, and systems knowledge to build software solutions for users. They often oversee and manage all aspects of a project from beginning to end. This branch of computer science deals with software design, development, maintenance, testing, and application. Various roles within the broader software engineering category include front-end or back-end engineers, full-stack engineers, quality assurance engineers, security engineers, and mobile app developers.

Top-general skills

One of the most important general software engineering skills is communication. It involves the ability to give and receive feedback with clarity and understanding. Good communication skills also allow engineers to collaborate effectively with colleagues and clients. Working well in a team is another important general skill for a software engineer. The ability to work collaboratively and leverage years of software engineering experience help software companies grow and achieve results. Another essential skill for software engineers is problem-solving. Strong problem-solving skills involve combining creative and analytical thinking, breaking down the question into smaller parts, and applying it to a problem using a systematic approach.

IT-specific skills

Computer programming and coding, software development, and object-orientated design are the most important IT skills relevant to becoming a software engineer. Which includes proficiency in programming languages such as Python, Java, C, C++, or Scala. Some engineers may focus on database development and algorithms, while others might specialise in testing procedures. Other IT skills involve experience with platforms to share and develop code, such as GitHub, and bug-checking software, such as Jira.



Security Consultant

A security consultant is tasked with protecting a client's data from being breached while also identifying problems in the business's software. A security consultant or analyst pinpoints vulnerabilities in computer systems, networks, and software programs and works closely with clients and organisations to develop strategic approaches to strengthen and future-proof IT systems. Which may involve regular security audits and assessments of an organisation's hardware, network, and software systems. A security consultant is often engaged as an independent expert to review and provide advice and strategy for IT security.

Top-general skills

Important general skills for being a security consultant include strong problem-solving skills, having the ability to define a problem, and using analytical thinking to find creative ways to solve it. Strong communication and interpersonal skills are needed to liaise with clients to understand how their systems work and identify specific vulnerabilities or risks. Other general skills include risk assessment and management, incident and report writing, and understanding cybersecurity's key concepts and language. This role requires strong analytical skills, collaboration, teamwork, and attention to detail.

IT-specific skills

The IT-specific skills required for a security consultant are a strong understanding of a range of hardware networks and software systems with the ability to test and problem-solve in these various systems. A security consultant must know the current standards and regulations for IT systems, pinpoint vulnerabilities in computer systems, networks, and software programs, and identify strategies to mitigate risk and overcome these vulnerabilities. It may involve setting up and implementing user access controls and access management systems, monitoring network and application performance, identifying irregular activity, and performing audits to ensure security practices are compliant. Sometimes, it might involve deploying endpoint detection and prevention tools to prevent hacking and malicious attacks. Knowledge of encryption technologies and procedures for handling security incidents is also important.
