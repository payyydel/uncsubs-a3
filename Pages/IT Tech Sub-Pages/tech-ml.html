Machine Learning

Joshua Peddell


What does it do?

“Machine Learning” (ML) is a branch of “Artificial Intelligence” (AI). To understand ML, you first must understand that AI's goal is to computationally imitate and mimic human thought processing speeds and intelligence. ML’s purpose, being a subset of AI, is for programs and machines to learn and adapt without the need for human input. Multifaceted algorithms are built into hardware and/or software with the purpose of recognising data and building predictions surrounding it. A more profound subset of ML, termed “Deep Learning” (DL), applies a more efficient artificial neural network method for processing large quantities of “unstructured” and “structured” data. Examples of unstructured data are natural language text, speech, images, and videos, whereas examples of structured data are names, credit card numbers, geolocation, and stock information.

Currently, ML is present in our everyday lives more than people probably realise. It has ushered a means for systems to enrich their knowledge base with larger data sets, allowing for fewer programming and logic errors across the industry.

Two key examples of this are image and speech recognition. The most recognisable uses of ML to date, image and speech recognition, have been adapted in the field of pattern recognition. A significant milestone in the overall pursuit of AI. Real-world applications include handwriting recognition, subject classification, voice search, dictation recognition, appliance control etc. Social media platforms such as Facebook use ML algorithms to compile your activity, likes, comments, friends, locational check-ins and more to create targeted advertisements, suggest friends and events, recommend group communities and more. Consumer-facing technology giants like Apple use their A-Series chips in their iPhones and M-Series chips in their iPads and Mac products to power their ML functions such as image and video object detection, language analysis, sound classification, and of course, their “smart” assistant Siri. Relentlessly adapting based on new data, ML continues to evolve. Relatively speaking, this technology is only in its infancy.

Therefore prospectively, short-term progressions likely to be made will be the development and implementation of hyper-automation, the beginnings of a quantum leap in cyber security, and the evolutionary introduction of the “Metaverse”.

Hyper-automation is an enterprise-driven, methodical approach with the intent of automating as many business and IT-related processes as possible. Applying Robotic Process Automation (RPA) to lessen human dependency allows for increased accuracy, consistency and speed overall, effectively reducing any losses typically incurred by human error or oversight. A corporate surge in the employment of RPA technologies is imminent within the next three to five years and will predicatively reduce operational costs by up to 30%.

The cyber security industry is booming as of late. With the progression of technology comes increased vulnerability, and with a globally estimated cost of $6 trillion annually due to cybercrimes, businesses have already begun to explore AI/ML options to alleviate risks. Employing AI and ML-powered patterns and unusual activity recognition in an effort to not only automate but diminish risks. Thereby dissuading sceptical investors and restoring confidence in businesses. A unique advantage to AI/ML power cyber security is its ability to accumulate and adapt with precision and unparalleled speed.

Originally announced at Facebook, now Meta’s “Connect 2021” keynote event, the “Metaverse” is set to be a revolutionary step towards global connection, essentially evolving and absorbing the internet. Serving as a fundamental foundation for the venture, AI and ML will power this Augmented Reality (AR)/Virtual Reality (VR) platform. Aiding as a vital linking component of major technologies involved like 3D and VR animation, and automating and improving repetitive tasks, thus increasing efficiency across the entire platform.



What is the likely impact?

ML’s likely impact as we advance will be the implementation of it, as well as AI, across almost every facet of human existence. From enterprise to day-to-day, the application of ML will create opportunities by helping block out the white noise felt in everyday tasks, allowing for a more focused approach towards our evolution. Significant likely everyday impacts will be in the realms of education and smart home security.

ML can provide a revolutionary approach to how we are educated going forward. With the implementation of "smart classrooms", systems would be able to record an individual’s performance in select areas and formulate a highly effective study plan designed around empowering students, cultivating their strengths, as well as a means by which to strengthen any necessary shortcomings required in either chosen career path or fundamental subjects. This would, in turn, ease the burden on educators and produce productive members of society. It would even aid in reducing mental health issues typically a result of a stagnated learning experience in early adolescence, improving people’s quality of life.

ML power smart home security systems are becoming a part of the norm, providing peace of mind to homeowners and residents. Essential smart home security features include facial recognition, sound analysis, speech recognition, smoke detection and more. ML smart home security systems provide a level of automation and recognition that could mean the difference between losing your belongings due to a fire or a break-in and even life and death. It would allow for home and contents premiums to be reduced thanks to increased response times and reduced crime rates in residential suburbs.

Overall, ML is likely to affect a large number of the population. Increased efficiency and effectiveness on a global scale will make problem-solving a much less time-consuming endeavour. It will make way for an evolutionary quantum leap in technology. Though it would make a significant number of jobs redundant, it would open up a whole realm of employment opportunities, raising the bar moving forward for employment.



How will this affect you?

In my everyday life, I use a number of different services, platforms, and hardware that all have some manner of ML to them. The aforementioned progression can undoubtedly improve an individual’s day-to-day quality of life. I foresee a progressive change in how I go about tasks, with most of the mundane ones being relatively automated. Increased efficiency and accuracy would aid in transport predictions and planning, work processes, and my home security and automation use, generally just allowing me to focus on what’s important.

This will have a very positive impact on members of my family. In an ideal world, my kid brother would have the opportunity to study in a manner that feels intuitive and effortless. It would make university feel like less of a trial of impending adulthood and more like a standard of moral excellence to hold himself to, a place for him to be challenged in an inviting community. My mother would be able to visit her family overseas or attend important cultural events that she would otherwise be unable to participate in via some means of AR/VR. My father and other younger brother would be able to work in their chosen trades with ease and efficiency thanks to even the seemingly minuscule technological improvements in their relevant fields.

ML is an all-encompassing technology, pioneering a path for all manners of technologies. However, I do have some reservations I’d be remiss not to mention. I am all for the progression of technology; however, I'm worried about the immediate impacts such a rapidly evolving technology would have upon noticeable, widespread impact. As for everyday life, the adjustment period will be rough and must be handled swiftly but delicately. As with most of the population, we’ll likely miss doing some things ourselves, with our hands, in a manner of speaking. But I do think these kinds of responses could lead to an equilibrium of sorts. Balancing technology and our humanity.
