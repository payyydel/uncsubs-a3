Chief Executive Officer

Generally typecast as the leader, the CEO will be responsible for the company's success, as well as its failures. Playfully considered "The Dreamer", the CEO will bring passion and belief in the core idea of the platform, what it stands for, and what it can do. They will be responsible for the day-to-day operations as well as long-term growth. The CEO will have a legally binding, ethical duty to ensure all company practices meet industry standards ethically; they will coordinate all teams with a stoic and passionate sense of leadership, as well as partner with the CPO in vendor pursuits for opening subscription service offers.

Skills requirements will include superior communication skills in all aspects of the job, a tangible leadership style and presence, decisive and informed decision-making abilities, especially under pressure and teambuilding and teamwork skills to keep everyone on task and working in tandem towards the same vision.



Chief Product Officer

The CPO will be the tactical leader of the company, responsible for the execution of the product/platform from early development to launch and beyond. They will need to coordinate with others to ensure goals are aligned and helm the early stages of marketing. Additionally, a significant responsibility will be reaching out to vendors, including all manner of subscription-based services for the company to partner with. They will need to build up an impressive roster of partnerships for launch, without which the company will ultimately fail.

Skill requirements will include relative technical skills to create prototypes; marketing abilities will be heavily intertwined with the partnership efforts; communication skills, business acumen to launch the product effectively; and sturdy strategical thinking skills. The ability to provide insight and predictions based on a potential chain of events set into motion by singular actions will be invaluable.



Chief Technology Officer

Responsible for the company's technological and research and development needs, the CTO will prospectively lead all technical and engineering-oriented departments. Examining short and long-term needs, the CTO will be responsible for developing procedures and policies and using their technological expertise to enhance services and products to align with business goals and the overall betterment of customers. Given the nature of the start-up, the CTO will have their technical capabilities flexed a lot as they, along with the CPO, will need to create a workable prototype of the platform.

Skill requirements will include in-depth technical knowledge and expertise, front and backend software development skills, keen business acumen for overall effectiveness across the business, strong communication skills, a proven leadership capability and exceptional troubleshooting and problem-solving skills ranging from technical to business.



Chief Sales Officer

The CSO will be responsible for all sales-based activities for the company. They will be collaborating side by side with the CPO to strategise a product roll-out plan and means to non-invasively monetise the service to turn a profit and support further growth, development, and upkeep. In essence, they will build and navigate an ever-growing marketplace with the goal of driving real-world results ultimately towards our bottom line.

Skill Requirements will include strong verbal and nonverbal communication skills, prominent leadership abilities, variable strategical thinking capabilities, an aptitude for analytical problem-solving, the ability to see the bigger picture, effective relationship-building skills and powerful sales abilities and experience.
