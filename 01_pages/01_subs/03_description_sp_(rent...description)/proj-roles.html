In this project, most members have roles that align with their strengths and interests. These roles were not explicitly defined and allocated; however, as the project progressed, members established themselves in specific positions.

Our Product Manager, the person who supervises all aspects of the project development process, is Omar. A Product Manager's range of responsibilities is quite diverse. Omar's duties during the project as Product Manager include controlling and planning tasks, leading, and facilitating meetings, collaborating with developers, and generating ideas. Omar also initiated the project idea. Omar has taken on other typical duties of a Product Manager during this process, including accepting and rejecting ideas and facilitating technical documentation and maintenance.

In this role, a Business Analyst acts as a bonding agent between the market and engineers, outlining the target audiences' needs and analysing their behaviour and concerns to pass this to the engineers. The role fits Kamille as her work in assignment one interviewing Kamille demonstrated interest in risk management, group processes and communication. She brings strong organisational, analytical and communication skills, which are all vital responsibilities of a Business Analyst as this role suits someone comfortable working and liaising with a wide range of people.

Another essential role in a software development team is a Tester, who handles the manual part of the testing stage, reflecting the users operating habits and patterns. The role has been assigned to Matt because Matthew has shown interest in testing, verification, and work in this area.

The Engineering Manager of the team is Josh. As a manager, this role works with every team member and understands the teams' performance dynamics well. As Engineering Manager, Josh provides coaching and answers any software questions the team may have. The Engineering Manager also optimises the software development team structure when new developers join the team. This role requires a solid technological background and confidence in choosing the best engineering solutions, both of which Josh has consistently shown throughout his work.

Clyde will take on the role of User Interface/User Experience (UI/UX) designer in our team. This role helps create user-friendly interfaces that ensure users can use the product or service. It is a good role for Clyde because he is passionate about the latest technology trends and devices and likes working with the end users.

Finally, Molly is taking on the role of Quality Assurance or QA Engineer. This role requires attention to detail in creating and running tests and ensuring the product is free from bugs and issues. The QA Engineer works closely with the Engineering Manager and Tester to ensure the product is ready to launch.
