Our Platform

The fundamental function of our project idea is to improve the accessibility of subscription-based entertainment streaming services to all users by creating a centralised hub for payments. Building upon that also acts as a primary hub for accessing the content the users are paying for, whether audio or video-based services. The services that our platform will have available will have complete coverage to not only incorporate entertainment streaming subscription services like Disney+, Netflix and Spotify, to name a few but also learning subscription services such as Skill-Share, Masterclass and for book lovers, audiobook services such as Audible and Blinkist among others. Our platform will be a uniquely diversified CenterPoint for a wide range of services that is unprecedented.



How will we secure these partnerships?

Partnerships with streaming subscription services will be integral to our business model. We will not only require our platform to be a payment option through the subscription services platforms but also allow us to access and display their content on our platform. The way we would secure these partnerships would be to form business relationships by organising meetings and discussing the goals we have for our company and what ideas we have that will significantly benefit their customers and their companies (Masterclass 2021). Agreements would need to be put into place that would detail many important clauses regarding how we would promote their services, how profits would be shared, and each company's responsibility to each other in the partnership, to name a few.



What will it do?

The platform our team is designing will allow users to have full functionality of their favourite services from one convenient location. This ease of access will be available from our web application as well as our mobile app. Users of our platform will be able to subscribe to as many of the partnered streaming services as they wish. If a service is no longer required, users can unsubscribe from any of the services with the same ease as it was to subscribe in the first place. This allows users to only pay for what they need and when they need it. The appeal of this feature is that it will save money on unutilised subscriptions.

To continue adding to our platform's convenience, users can view or listen to the content of their subscribed services using our embedded media player. This is where users can choose any content from their subscriptions to enjoy on our platform in one convenient location. The platform interface will have a list of tiles displaying the options for the content of the subscripted services the user has to choose from. This will vary from user to user as the services are ultimately left up to the user to select.



How will we program this?

We will design a user interface that is user-friendly and visually pleasing. This will need to be designed and implemented across multiple languages such as Object C, Java, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS to allow for a consistent UI experience when cross-platforming between iOS, Android, and Web browsers.



User Recommendations

With machine learning, we will gather information on our users to improve our user experience by recommending new entertainment and subscription options based on the content determined by the users' history. The way the algorithm will decide what to recommend will be based not only on history but other variables such as playtime, category, and repetition, which will include continuously played episodes of a particular series, whether it be audio or video. A more generalised recommendation list will also be made available where more significant amounts of data will be collected by all users, which will suggest the most popular content based on the user's location and gender.



How will we display these recommendations?

Recommendations will be displayed on the home screen of our platform, where the subscriptions that users have subscribed to are displayed. This will have a unique user interface, as it will open the doors to all the subscription options available by displaying content of all the options available from one place. This will serve two purposes preventing the user from feeling as if they have to navigate multiple subscriptions, thereby increasing user experience satisfaction and allowing for that grandstand type feeling of a giant hub of content which will also serve the same purpose.



Registration

The registration process that users of our platform will experience will be aligned with the main principle of our platform, which is ease and simplicity. Users will start the sign-up process like any other platform by entering their nominated email address and creating a password. From there, users will be given the option to select their preferred payment method, such as direct debit via debit or credit card.



How will we enable these payment options?

Payment options will be supported methodically. Beginning by creating a registered business. This can be done through an accounting firm registering our business details and providing us with an Australian Business Number and an Australian Company Number. This process will legally allow our business to operate in Australia and pay the appropriate taxes.

With the use of the ABN and ACN, we will then secure a business merchant banking account and a payment gateway. Most banks in Australia offer merchant accounts that are packaged together with payment gateways. A payment gateway is necessary as it is a secure service for websites and applications to receive payments, which acts like a digital card reader.

Users will have to agree to have their payment information saved on our platform. Once payment information is secured, our platform will confirm the payment details by making a small charge to the users' nominated payment option. Users will then be asked to provide evidence of these transactions by telling us precisely the amounts charged. As soon as this confirmation has been completed, the confirmation charges will be reversed, being processed immediately. This will allow for monthly billing and allow for ease of adding new services to their subscription.



What security purpose would this serve?

A platform that is based on payments and user information so heavily, it is undoubtedly expected that security is an extremely important factor for us to consider. This is the reason for the confirmation of the payment method. The process we require our users to go through provides proof of ownership of the bank account related to the payment method they provide, reducing the risk of fraud.



How will we secure their personal information?

To further ensure the security of our users' information, it will be necessary and expected by our users that we have all their payment and personal information that we keep encrypted for their safety. We will ensure this by implementing a Security Sockets Layer internet security protocol on our platform. Our team will do this by obtaining an SSL Certificate. With the use of SSL, we will ensure that our user's information is safe with 256-bit encryption.



Choice of Services

After finalising registration and payment, users will select which services they would like to subscribe to. Once a service is selected, users will be directed to the service provider's linked website. This is where users will have the option to either sign up for that service; or, if it is a service that is already a subscription held by the user, in both instances, they will have the option to choose their payment preference to be charged through our platform. This will be made possible by the partnerships our platform will have gained with the services that are then made available to our users.



Payment

We expect that many of the users of our platform will already have a hand full of subscriptions to some, if not many, of the popular streaming services. That's why, again, we have made the transition to our platform as convenient as possible, thanks to the partnerships that will be established with the streaming services we offer. Should users join our platform at any point through their current subscription period, the transition period will be charged at a pro-rata rate. For instance, a user has three subscription services they are currently paying for, billed on the 2nd,15th, and 29th of the month. The user will select the preferred payment date for their combined subscription on our platform, which may be the 30th of each month.

Rather than being billed the total amount, users will be billed at a pro-rata rate for the duration of the month. This will be calculated by calculating the monthly fee over 30 days as a percentage and then taking away the percentage value of the days left in the month. For a $10 subscription billed from the 2nd to the 30th, the user will be charged $9.24. For a $15 subscription billed from the 15th to the 30th, the user will be charged $7.50, and for a $20 subscription billed from the 29 to the 30th, the user will be billed $0.66. This will allow all the users to merge the accounts for the duration of the month. Marketing

As with any successful product, our team must create and implement a strong marketing strategy. There are opportunities to take a more modern approach, as attention has dramatically shifted towards digital devices. It is no secret that as social media have evolved, attention spans have drastically reduced. Our team has identified the importance of embracing and using this understanding to create a marketing campaign based on social media and fast-paced content.

Media analysts and Data Scientists have identified that the typical attention span of social media consumers is an average of 8 seconds (Kraus Group Marketing 2022). Therefore, our strategy is to design advertising that communicates our message within this time frame. This aligns with our platform's "no time to waste" values and is marketing to the demographic of people who are time constrained and will likely see the most benefit in consolidating their entertainment with our platform.



Tell us about the ''story'' of your project – how it began, how it has progressed, and what stage of the plan you are up to.

The origin of our project idea started during assessment 2. We had to collectively decide whose project idea we would use as a team. We all had shown each other's project idea to every team member and concluded that Omar's original project idea would be what we used for assessments 2 and 3.

Our earliest project idea was to be the creation of an application/website where consumers can pay for their subscription services, such as Netflix, Amazon, Spotify etc., on a centralised platform so that it reflects as a single payment rather than multiple. All subscriptions will charge via the service as per their regular billing date, culminating in a single charge from a chosen bank account or credit/debit card. Simply select the subscription service you desire, then add it to your account to be billed each month, and you can choose any service as you please. You will be able to manage all your subscriptions in one place. You'll be able to access the platform on any iOS and Android device. And any web browser (excluding internet explorer).

This was the project's original idea that Omar had created; however, we saw a greater potential here. Considering that the project idea was to create a more manageable platform for managing payments for subscriptions so users did not have to manage the individual services and payments coming out at different times of the month. We also wanted to incorporate the streaming service aspect into the platform, becoming an all-in-one design. In contrasting hindsight, the original idea had that element of convenience, not having to manage each subscription; however, you still had to log onto their website and watch their show there.

Our primary motivation and goal were saving the user time and ease of use for all apps and platforms. This saves users from having numerous logins for multiple platforms, right? And having to access multiple websites and even not requiring having multiple apps anymore.

We are developing the project idea further in the implementation stage, as this will be a future update/content for our users. An exciting endgame goal of ours is exploring the option of further implementing utility bills where you can assign what bills you want to come out within the same timeframe. Furthermore, considering the billing nature of quarterly utility bills, you would be unable to place them into a month-to-month billing system. We will give you the power to group up the bills that come out within the same timeframe as each other to be detracted from your account automatically. It's a more rigid system to implement because not all bills are monthly, as they would have to align with each other.



Include any dead-ends you may have followed, decisions made, and changes that have been made to the project plan. This will need to include a significant amount of detail so that it is easily seen what you have done and are planning to do.

Initially, the project idea was to create a centralised platform through which users would keep track of subscription payments; however, over time, the concept developed to include a broader range of integrated subscription services. This project idea eventually evolved into a service where users stream their services via our platform instead of managing payments. The concept was then developed to integrate machine learning into our platform using an AI algorithm to receive data on what the users are watching—achieved through surveys to build a profile about the user through their data and what this says about their behaviour and preferences.

We then realised this data collected could also be used to provide more targeted product-based subscriptions through a different segment of the app and website. We then introduced the facility, which will feature products from a range of companies that use a subscription-based model and enables users to browse and subscribe monthly subscriptions to trusted companies such as Scratch, Athletic Greens or Hello Fresh.

A "dead-end idea" we had to scrap was "goodie boxes". Initially, we planned to include monthly subscriptions to these boxes, which we wouldn't sell as we would only feature pre-existing products already available to customers, such as candy care packages or beauty and health care boxes. Initially, implementing this feature would save time and hassle for users already subscribed to product-based companies. However, after further thought, we decided not to follow this direction. We wanted to focus our efforts on the core product and platform without introducing physical products. Introducing physical products might also be a distraction from the primary business. In this way, we made UNC Subs an exclusively digital platform that helps focus our vision and strategy.

Testing

The testing of an application is best conducted by taking a series of specific steps to ensure all the necessary functions of an application run effectively and to the highest degree of security possible. It is vital to the development process to ensure that all test measures have been taken in order to deliver a product that is operating as intended prior to its release.


Test Process

In order to sufficiently test our software application, our team will follow testing procedures and methods, as well as make use of an IaaS. The processes that we will follow will consist of the following steps. Functional testing this is conducted by giving inputs to the individual functions and checking the results of the output without considering the coding composition. Usability testing can be performed by our team or external users. To find external users, we will make use of services such as Apple's TestFlight application.

Interface testing guarantees that the activity between application server interfaces and web server is seamless, including ensuring error alerts are adequately visualised. Compatibility testing, as our software will be available on all web browsers (excluding explorer), Android and iOS, we will need to test across all these platforms to certify identical functionality is available.

Using an IaaS such as Real Device Cloud will be a great way to access over 3000 real devices and browsers where testing can be carried out manually or automatically. Different Operating Systems will also need to be tested to ensure APIs, interface calls, and designs are compatible. Performance testing will be carried out after ensuring compatibility. This will be done by stress testing our application through different internet speeds to monitor how it will handle regular operation and peak loads. We will push the application past its limit to find the breaking point to ensure it will not fail its users once made available.

Security testing is the final step in our testing process. Our team will test the access to the secure pages to make sure that unauthorised access is not possible, restricted files are unable to be accessed by unauthorised parties and ensure user inactivity results in open sessions being closed. Following a process such as this will cover all aspects of the software, ensuring a stable, reliable and safe product for all its users.


Test Method

As Up & Coming develop our application, we will adhere to the Waterfall methodology. The advantages of this method are the steps of the project are completed in sequential order. Each phase of the development has particular deliverables and analysis process making it easier for our team to understand the requirements at every stage of the project. This will make it easy to know when we have thoroughly and successfully tested our software application.

Risks

Creating our project from scratch has its risks. Early identification of these risks, however, will help us to mitigate these risks and find solutions for issues that are likely to arise. One of the risks we identified is compatibility between platforms. We will be using a Mac-based operating system, and some functions may be affected on Windows devices.

We will be using Proto.io to create the prototype, although, upon further research on the app, we have found that the importation of files can be prone to errors and may corrupt files. We are also unfamiliar with this software, and it will take time to understand the interface. It could also be difficult to integrate other apps to create our product’s interface and functions. The app is also relatively expensive which may cause the project prototype creation to be unsustainable.

One of the integral parts of our project is being able to process multiple transactions as one payment. Secure Pay has a good reputation for being customisable and easy to integrate, however, it is possible that errors might occur combining subscription models.

According to Tripwire, most security breaches happen while data is in transit. While most payment gateways are secured through encryption between the website and the payment processing system, it is still at risk once the data reaches its destination (Tripwire, 2018). Tripwire (2018) cites poor security architecture and management, failure to encrypt information such as names, addresses and phone numbers and a sole focus on protecting credit card information as common reasons for data breaches. We must be mindful of these risks as they not only endanger our future customers but will also damage our product's reputation.

Our product requires integration with the streaming platforms and being able to process subscription payments from their members. There is a risk that we may not be able to get approval for these platforms. If we are able to partner with these streaming services, we can verify whether they are a valid user of the streaming service. If we do get partnerships approved, our users might find that streaming through our app is slower than watching or listening to their favourite programs through the services' native app.

These are some of the risks that we have identified. Though we have not started developing our project, it is vital to develop the ability to identify the issues we might face in the future.

The career website "Indeed" emphasises the importance of being able to pre-empt software risks as it is crucial to creating a quality product and meeting our business goals. The Indeed Editorial Team also states that we can mitigate the risks by ensuring that we test our codes frequently, resolve bugs and logical errors the moment they are found, and set high coding standards for our team.
